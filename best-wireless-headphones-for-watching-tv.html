FAQ

Q: How do I connect wireless headphones to my TV?

Bluetooth is the most common way to wirelessly connect. Simply put your headphones in pairing mode, which usually involves pressing the power button until you get a blinking LED or hear a beep.

Following this, you need to go to your TV settings, followed by sound or audio settings. Go to “sound out” and select “use wireless speaker.” Click on “Bluetooth device,” then find the name of your headphones and connect. Your headphones and TV should now be paired. However, these are general guidelines, and steps may vary from TV to TV.

If your TV is an older model, it may not have a Bluetooth option. In that case, you’ll need to invest in a Bluetooth receiver. Connect it to your TV's audio output, then pair your headphones.

If you have bought a set of headphones with an RF transmitter, you can connect it to your TV’s audio output or 3.5mm headphone jack and then adjust the TV volume to the desired level. Turn on your headphones and adjust their volume, too.

Q: Can I use any pair of wireless headphones with my TV?

If you have headphones and a TV with Bluetooth, then yes. However, older or budget-friendly TVs may not have Bluetooth capability. In that case, you will need a Bluetooth receiver or headphones with an RF transmitter.

Q: What is active noise cancelation?

ANC is a function that blocks out external noise. Headphones that utilize this technology have built-in mics and speakers that work in a sort of “anti-noise” way to block out unwanted sounds. They are effective with low frequencies but less effective with higher ones.

Not all headphones have ANC. Some have passive noise isolation. This usually involves thicker ear cups or snug tips to block external interference. It’s a more straightforward, less effective way that is reflected in the cost of the cans.

Q: How do I fix audio latency with my headphones?

If you are using Bluetooth headphones, you can simply try the old on/off trick. Unpairing and repairing the headphones may well be all you need to do.

If it doesn’t work, check if your TV and headphones use the same Bluetooth codec. You can find this information in the Bluetooth section of your TV’s settings. Your headphones’ manual will have the information on which codecs they support. Adjust accordingly and have the codecs match. Choosing the right codec is important, and you should choose the best low-latency option.

Some TVs also feature audio delay adjustment. Look for options like “audio sync” or “AV delay." You can adjust until the sound and picture match. You can also download apps like "Bluetooth Audio Delay” or “TV Assist,” which might prove helpful if your TV’s settings are of limited use.

Other troubleshooting methods include minimizing the distance between the TV and your headphones, updating any drivers and firmware on both your TV and headphones, and turning off any other Wi-Fi and Bluetooth devices you are not currently using.
