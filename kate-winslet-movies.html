Kate Winslet Movies, Ranked | First For Women

Publish date: 2024-05-09

Kate Winslet, the iconic British actress, has for decades graced the silver screen with her extraordinary talent and enthralling movies. On March 3rd, she will be starring in the 6-episode limited series, The Regime, premiering on HBO and Max.

The Regime is a dark comedy that tells the story of one year within the walls of the palace of a modern European regime as it begins to fall apart. Along with Winslet, lining up the cast are Belgian Rust and Bone heartthrob Matthias Schoenaerts, Guillaume Gallienne, Andrea Riseborough, Martha Plimpton and Hugh Grant.

But before we dive into Winslet’s newest endeavor, we’re taking a look back at some of her favorite roles over the years. Born in 1975 in Reading, England, Winslet began her acting career at an early age. At 11 she did her first commercial for a kids’ cereal. She started acting lessons around the same time, which led to formal training at a performing arts high school.

Over the next few years, she appeared on stage regularly and landed a few bit parts in sitcoms. Her big break came at age 17, when she was cast as an obsessive adolescent in Heavenly Creatures (1994). The film, based on the true story of two girls who commit a brutal murder, received modest distribution but was roundly praised by critics. Since that film, Winslet has become one of the most acclaimed and versatile actresses in the industry.

Winslet’s ability to portray a wide range of characters, coupled with her dedication to her craft, has earned her numerous accolades, including an Academy Award, three BAFTA Awards, and a Grammy Award.

Kate Winslet’s filmography is a testament to her versatility, dedication, and ability to bring characters to life with depth and authenticity. From her famous role in Titanic to her Oscar-winning performance in The Reader, Winslet continues to captivate audiences with her compelling portrayals.

Best Kate Winslet movies, ranked

We ranked our favorite Kate Winslet movies. Let us know if you agree with us.

14. The Mountain Between Us (2017)

The Mountain Between Us is a survival drama that follows two strangers, a journalist (Winslet) and a surgeon (Idris Elba), stranded in the wilderness after a plane crash. As they face harsh conditions, they form a bond that transcends the challenges they encounter. The movie is directed by Hany Abu-Assad.

13. Carnage (2011) Kate Winslet movies

Roman Polanski directs this drama starring Jodie Foster and John Reilly. Winslet portrays Nancy Cowan, one of two couples who meet to discuss a playground altercation between their sons, leading to a chaotic and revealing evening. Winslet, as usual, gives a captivating performance.

12. Quills (2000) Kate Winslet movies

Directed by Philip Kaufman, Quills is a historical drama inspired by the life of the Marquis de Sade. Winslet plays Madeleine ‘Maddy’ LeClerc, a laundress who becomes entangled in the controversial writings of Marquis (Geoffrey Rush). Joaquin Phoenix is also in the movie.

11. The Dressmaker (2015) Kate Winslet movies

Set in 1950s Australia, The Dressmaker features Winslet as Myrtle ‘Tilly’ Dunnage, a skilled dressmaker who returns to her hometown seeking revenge. The film blends dark comedy, drama, and fashion in a unique and visually stunning narrative. The movie also co-stars Liam Hemsworth and Judy Davis.

10. Steve Jobs (2015) Kate Winslet movies

In this biographical drama directed by Danny Boyle, Winslet takes on the role of Joanna Hoffman, a marketing executive and confidante to Apple co-founder Steve Jobs (Michael Fassbender). The film explores Jobs’ life and the evolution of Apple Inc. It also stars Seth Rogen.

9. Little Children (2006)

Directed by Todd Field, Little Children is a drama that delves into the lives of suburban parents. Winslet plays Sarah Pierce, a discontented housewife who finds herself drawn to a stay-at-home dad (Patrick Wilson), leading to a series of unexpected events. The movie also stars Jennifer Connelly.

8. Finding Neverland (2004)

In this biographical film, Winslet portrays Sylvia Llewelyn Davies, a widow whose family becomes the inspiration for J.M. Barrie’s play Peter Pan. The film explores the power of imagination and the bonds that form between Barrie (Johnny Depp) and the Davies family.

7. Revolutionary Road (2008)

Reuniting with Leonardo DiCaprio, Winslet plays April Wheeler in this intense drama directed by Sam Mendes. The film explores the disillusionment of a suburban couple as they grapple with unfulfilled dreams and societal expectations in 1950s America. The on-screen chemistry between the two leads make this a captivating film to watch.

6. The Reader (2008)

Starring opposite Ralph Fiennes, Winslet plays a former SS guard who defends herself in a war crime trial. Based on Bernhard Schlink’s novel, The Reader explores post-war guilt, redemption, and the consequences of personal choices. Winslet delivers a moving performance.

5. Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind (2004) Kate Winslet movies

Costarring Jim Carrey and Mark Ruffalo, this drama features Winslet as Clementine, a woman who undergoes a procedure to erase memories of her failed relationship with Joel (Jim Carrey). The film explores the complexities of love, memory, and human connection. Her performance in Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind is a side of acting we haven’t seen from Winslet before.

4. Sense and Sensibility (1995)

In this adaptation of Jane Austen’s classic novel, Winslet portrays Marianne Dashwood, one of the Dashwood sisters, facing romantic challenges in 19th-century England. The film, directed by Ang Lee, explores love, societal expectations, and the pursuit of happiness. Winslet played alongside other greats, including Emma Thompson and Hugh Grant.

3. Heavenly Creatures (1994) Kate Winslet movies

Directed by Peter Jackson, Heavenly Creatures is a psychological drama based on the true story of the Parker-Hulme murder case in 1950s New Zealand. Kate Winslet, in her debut role, plays Juliet Hulme, a teenage girl whose intense friendship with Pauline Parker (Melanie Lynskey) takes a dark turn. This is the film that introduced the world to Winslet’s acting prowess.

2. Titanic (1997)

James Cameron’s epic romance Titanic became a cultural phenomenon, and Winslet’s portrayal of Rose DeWitt Bukater alongside Leonardo DiCaprio’s Jack Dawson became iconic. The film chronicles the ill-fated maiden voyage of the RMS Titanic. The on-scene chemistry between Winslet and DiCaprio led to a real-life deep friendship.

1. The Holiday (2006)

This fun-filled romantic comedy, directed by Nancy Meyers, follows two women, Iris (Winslet) and Amanda (Cameron Diaz), who swap homes for the holidays to escape their respective relationship woes. Winslet brings warmth and humor to her role as Iris. The Holiday also stars the adorable Jude Law and Jack Black. This comedy is Winslet at her best.
