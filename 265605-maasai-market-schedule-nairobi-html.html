When art and culture mix, the result is always a masterpiece. The Maasai are one of the most popular tribes in East Africa inhabiting the Northern Tanzania and Southern Kenya. Apart from residing close to National Parks, the Maasai have one of richest culture in Kenya. It is no wonder, Maasai Market Kenya got its name from this tribe. Nevertheless, the Maasai Market in Nairobi is a culturally diverse and showcases the best collection of jewellery, artifacts, clothes, shoes, handbags, wall hangings, and utensils. As such, other than the signature Maasai bead, shoes, and cloth, you can find a collection of other creations from the different tribes in Kenya including stone carvings from the Kisii and wood carvings from the Kamba. Apart from being colorful, and unique, the products at the Maasai Market are quite affordable.

Tips for Shopping at Maasai Market Nairobi

Like in any market, you need to learn the ins and outs of the Maasai Market to get the best bargains and value for your money. Here are some of the tricks and tips for shoppers

Always go for quality; do not buy something because someone is selling to you. Examine the finishing and overall look. If it is not satisfactory move to the next trader until you find something that offers value for money.

Walk around the market first before you begin purchasing products. When you do so, you can sample the different products and prices before making a buying decision. Also, you stand a chance of getting the best bargain in the market.

Know what you want or need. You do not have to ask the prices of every item in the market if you are not purchasing.

Learn some basic Swahili. Knowledge of the local language works to your advantage when negotiating the prices and bargains.

Do not talk to brokers in the market. Though having a guide in the market may look like a good idea, it may cost more. Most of the brokers will increase the prices on the item to cater for their commissions.

Engage the sellers in the market. Small talk works to your benefit and may get you a good bargain. You can ask about the process of making the products among other things.

Ensure you have changed your money into Kenyan shillings.

Choose the market with the best prices and value. From experience, the Kijabe Street park and High Court car park markets have the best prices and bargains.

Do not go for the obvious tourist goods such as Safari T-shirts as their prices are slightly higher than the rest. Choose something that is unique and culturally rich.
