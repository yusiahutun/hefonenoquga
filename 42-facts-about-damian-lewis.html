Damian Lewis was born on February 11, 1971.

Born in St. John’s Wood, London, Lewis shares his birthday with other notable celebrities such as Jennifer Aniston and Taylor Lautner.

He graduated from the Guildhall School of Music and Drama.

Pursuing his passion for acting, Lewis honed his skills at the prestigious Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London, where he studied alongside renowned actors like Ewan McGregor and Joseph Fiennes.

Lewis is of Welsh and English descent.

His father, Watcyn Lewis, is Welsh, while his mother, Charlotte Mary Bowater, is of English descent. This mixed heritage has undoubtedly contributed to his diverse range of roles.

He made his West End debut in “Hamlet.”

Lewis made his mark in the theater world with his West End debut as Laertes in Shakespeare’s “Hamlet” at the Open Air Theatre in Regent’s Park.

Damian Lewis is known for his signature red hair.

With his fiery red hair, Lewis has become recognizable and often stands out in a crowd. It has become one of his distinguishing features as an actor.

He played Soames Forsyte in the miniseries “The Forsyte Saga.”

Lewis gained critical acclaim for his portrayal of Soames Forsyte, a complex and brooding character, in the acclaimed miniseries “The Forsyte Saga,” based on the novels by John Galsworthy.

Lewis starred in the hit series “Band of Brothers.”

One of his most memorable roles was as Major Richard Winters in the Emmy-winning miniseries “Band of Brothers,” produced by Steven Spielberg and Tom Hanks.

He has worked extensively in both film and television.

Throughout his career, Lewis has effortlessly transitioned between the big screen and television, showcasing his versatility as an actor.

Damian Lewis won an Emmy Award for his role in “Homeland.”

A career-defining moment for Lewis, he won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series for his portrayal of Nicholas Brody in the popular series “Homeland.”

He played Henry VIII in the miniseries “Wolf Hall.”

Lewis portrayed the infamous King Henry VIII in the critically acclaimed miniseries “Wolf Hall,” based on the historical novel by Hilary Mantel.

Lewis received an OBE for his services to drama.
