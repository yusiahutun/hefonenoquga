Francia Raisa shows off fit figure in sports bra and sweatpants while walking her pup in LA

Publish date: 2024-08-22

Francia Raisa took advantage of some downtime from her career as an actress, along with another sunny Southern California day, and headed out on a walk with her dog and a friend..

The Los Angeles native kept it sporty in the fashion department when she stepped out for her stroll with her four-legged family member in the Studio City neighborhood of Los Angeles on Sunday.

While Raisa, 34, is known for her roles in The How I Met Your Father, as well as Bring It On: All or Nothing, The Secret Life Of The American Teenager and Grown-ish, many fans also know her as the woman who selflessly donated one of her kidney's to friend Selena Gomez in her time of need in 2017.

The two longtime friends reportedly had a fallout of sorts late last year when Raisa felt slighted by Gomez when she called Taylor Swift her 'only friend in the industry.'

Seemingly offended by the comment, considering they had been friends for 15 years, Gomez and Raisa initially shaded one another online, but now nearly six months later, it appears they've worked things out and are pals once again.

Beaming: Francia Raisa, 34, was all smiles when she headed out for a walk with her dog in the Studio City neighborhood of Los Angeles on Sunday

Raisa, 34, was all smiles as she led her pet pooch around the suburban streets dressed in off-white sweatpants with a matching sports bra that helped showcase her toned abs.

She also wore a long white sweater at times but for the most part weight had it hanging off her shoulders or took it off altogether.

Her sporty outfit also included a pair of white sneakers and stylish dark sunglasses, 

Rounding out her look for the dog walk, Raisa had her dark red tresses styled long, straight and flowing down to about the middle of her back with a center part.

Raisa and Gomez first met during a visit to the Children's Hospital organized by Disney and ABC Family in 2007, and had been friends ever since. 

Some five years after Raisa donated her kidney to Gomez, their friendship would be put to the test when Gomez talked about feeling like an outcast in the music industry in a cover story for Rolling Stonein November 2022.

'I never fit in with a cool group of girls that were celebrities. My only friend in the industry really is Taylor [Swift], so I remember feeling like I didn't belong,' she confessed during the interview, while never mentioning Raisa.

Taking the comment personally, the actress wrote 'Interesting' under an Instagram post highlighting Gomez’s quote, and then proceeded to unfollow her the day after the premiere of her new documentary My Mind And Me.

Gift of life: While Raisa is known for her role in The How I Met Your Father,, many fans also know her as the woman who selflessly donated one of her kidney's to friend Selena Gomez in her time of need in 2017; they are pictured in the hospital

Gomez left an eyebrow raising comment underneath a TikTok video that discussed how her former best friend had unfollowed her on Instagram. 

'Sorry I didn’t mention every person I know,' Gomez remarked in the comments.

On top of that, the Calm Down singer never made mention of Raisa in her documentary My Mind and Me, which details her struggles with mental health issues over the past six years.

Both ladies revealed that they 'went through depression' during the post-surgery phase, according to Self. 

The singer and actress reportedly had post-op complications, where her kidney turned around inside of her body, which ultimately resulted in her having emergency surgery to correct it.

The situation got scary enough for Gomez that she sent Raisa a text confessing: 'I'm so scared I might die.'

Their rift: Raisa, an actress, was reportedly not happy to hear when Gomez said her only celebrity friend in the business is Taylor Swift last November, but it appears the two ladies have worked things out

But signs of a reconciliation between the two ladies appeared to be solidified when the former Disney star praised Raisa for donating her kidney amid her battle with lupus in an interview for Apple TV+’s “Dear... in March.

“My best friend. Her name is Francia. She said "No I am absolutely getting tested,"' Gomez recalled after finding out she was in need of an organ transplant. 

'Within three days, she went to [get tested], and she was a match. And it was one of those moments where I felt watched over. I know I was so so so lucky.'

She added, 'I will never, ever, ever be more in debt to a person than Francia. The idea of someone not even second-guessing to be a donor was unbelievably overwhelming.'
