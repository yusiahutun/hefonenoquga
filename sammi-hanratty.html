Professional Career

Sammi Hanratty started her acting journey with the 2005 soap opera “Passions” where she is seen playing the role of Adele in an episode of its sixth season. She continued to appear in TV shows such as “Charmed,” “Drake & Josh,” and “CSI: NY.” Moreover, she also appeared in the action drama series “The Unit” and the American sitcom “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.”

She came to prominence after playing the main role of Celia in the 2006 ABC family comedy-drama television film “Hello Sister, Goodbye Life.” Later, she also appeared in the 2006 TV film “Stephen King’s Desperation” as a supporting actress. 

Caption: The poster TV film Hello Sister, Goodbye Life (Source: IMDb)

She also appeared in her first feature film, “Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man’s Chest,” in 2006, playing a minor uncredited role. The film was directed by Gore Verbinski and was the sequel to the popular “Pirates of the Caribbean” film series. The film was a huge commercial success and is still one of the highest-grossing films of all time. She also appeared in the film “The Santa Claus 3: The Escape Claus” that same year.

She did the recurring role of Holly for 6 episodes of the American sitcom “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.” Her other recurring role was as Young Charlotte “Chuck” Charles in the comedy-drama series “Pushing Daisies” appearing in 5 episodes.

Continued to appear in films and television series

In 2007, she played the Young Laura in the horror film “Boogeyman 2.” During the same time, she also played a role in the TV series “Pushing Daisies” and her performance received critical acclaim. In 2008, she played a supporting role as Marley Singer in the thriller movie “Hero Wanted.”

In the 2009 film “An American Girl: Chrissa Stands Strong,” she played her first lead role as Chrissa Maxwell. It was the fifth film of the “American Girl” film series, directed by Martha Coolidge. In 2009, she co-directed the animated film “A Christmas Carol” with Oscar winner Robert Zemeckis. She provided the voices for several characters. The film was a commercial success.

In addition, she appeared in the low-budget film “Jack and the Beanstalk” the same year. Moreover, she had a supporting role in the psychological thriller film “Kalamity” the following year. She starred in the 2011 adventure film “The Greening of Whitney Brown.” She was cast in films such as “The Lost Medallion: The Adventures of Billy Stone” (2013), “Moms’ Night Out” (2014), “Bad Kids of Crestview Academy” (2017), and “Countdown” (2019).

Her other television series credit is “Monday Mornings” (2013), “Perception” (2014), “How to Get Away with Murder” (2015), “NCIS” and “Grand Hotel” (2019). She has been currently cast in the main role of Teen Misty Quigley in the thriller drama series “Yellowjackets.”
