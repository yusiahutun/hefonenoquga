Early life

The journalist was born on November 27, 1973, in Poughkeepsie, New York. She is of white ethnicity. The personality has not revealed details of her parents or siblings on public domains.

Cass was a great athlete, playing varsity soccer, softball, and volleyball while growing up. While in Korea, she studied tae kwon do, achieving her red belt. So where did Kelly Cass go to college? Kelly did her A.S. in Communications from Dutchess Community College.

She later got a B.A. in Communication from Adelphi University in Long Island. The personality then received a certificate of broadcast meteorology from Mississippi State University.

Career

Kelly pursued her passion in weather and meteorology and began her career in 1990. After graduation, she started as an associate producer and, later, a weeknight weather weathercaster at WTZA-TV (now WRNN), an independent station in Kingston, New York.

In December 1992, she went to WRGB-TV as a meteorologist, where she worked for seven years continuously. Besides that, Cass has also worked as Freelance On-Camera Meteorologist for WKRN-TV Nashville and WXIA-TV 11 Alive News.

Kelly then joined The Weather Channel, where she has worked for almost two decades. The personality works alongside Stephanie Abrams, Jen Carfagno, and Mike Bettes. She is enthusiastic about meteorology, which shows in her work.

Among Kelly's unique features while at The Weather Channel is that she included a segment about being picked for a flight in an F-A 18 jet of the United States Blue Angels unit. Her dedication to the field has earned her several awards.

So far, she has received the NWA Seal of Approval, the AMS Seal of Approval, and the Certificate of International Study in Seoul, South Korea. In addition, she is a full member of the American Meteorological Society with the Seal of Approval.
