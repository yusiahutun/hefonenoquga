For decades, country music has never shied away from producing murder ballads. Musicians release these subgenres of traditional ballads because they are the oldest form of crime literature. People usually listen to them because the songs are the first place to get cheap thrills based on a true story. Then, you have Kate McCannon, a murder ballad telling the story of a love gone wrong. But is Kate McCannon a true story?

Kate McCannon is a murder ballad by Canadian singer, songwriter, and musician Colter Wall. The song tells the story of a man behind bars giving a chronological order of events of how he met a beautiful girl called Kate McCannon, fell in love with her, and later killed her.

It has received praise, particularly because of Wall's deep voice and the dark side of the lyrics. Also, the song touches on issues that have always engulfed society, including infidelity and murder.

Is Kate McCannon a true story?

Kate McCannon is a song not based on a true story. Instead, it is a murder ballad that touches on the themes of anger and infidelity. Furthermore, the song is a testament to how infidelity and violence can easily entrap hardworking people in the lower class.

Traditionally, country musicians took stories from newspaper headlines to produce murder ballads. While most of those stories were true, they kept changing some things to get a good rhyme scheme. But, the songs were only partially true. However, one cannot deny that the stories were full of unpleasant truths and the tough reality that violence still exists in society.

That form of traditional play inspired Colter Wall to release Kate McCannon. However, the story sounds real because it is intense and compelling. Furthermore, Kate McCannon's real story appears true because cases of murder, where one partner finds another cheating and shoots them, have been common in society for many decades.

What is the Kate McCannon storyline?

Kate McCannon is a song sung from the perspective of a man serving time in prison. The man begins by explaining how a raven is a wicked bird floating outside his prison window. But in this context, the raven means the violence he sees around him and how it led to the murder of Kate McCannon. His story is more of a death-row confession, underscoring the immorality of the murder ballad genre.

Is Kate McCannon glorifying violence against women?
