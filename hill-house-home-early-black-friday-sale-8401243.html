The Internet-Famous Nap Dress I Practically Live In Is on Rare Sale

Publish date: 2024-08-03

There was an entire year where every time I opened Instagram, I was greeted by someone wearing a floor-length, ruffle-sleeved dress in the daintiest prints and most darling colors. That’s why this summer I finally gave in to the trend that doesn’t seem to be going anywhere: I bought Hill House Home’s internet-famous Nap Dress. And then I proceeded to basically live in it, only taking it off only to sleep (though, TBH, it was comfy enough for that too). 

The Nap Dress feels like if your favorite pajamas got a romantic, cottagecore makeover. If that’s not reason enough to buy it, it’s actually on rare sale right now. But it’s not just the viral dress that’s marked down — now through November 28, everything from the brand is 30 percent off with the code EVERYTHING30 as part of its Annual Sale. But given how quickly pieces go during this once-a-year sale, I don’t plan to wait to add things to my cart (and neither should you). 

Here’s everything I’m eyeing from Hill House Home’s sale, starting at $81:

The Ellie Nap Dress in Tartan

Obviously I’m eyeing another Ellie Nap Dress, which has a smocked bodice and flattering ruffle sleeves that work with bras, as well as a flowy, tiered bottom. “I look polished yet am so comfortable and get so many compliments,” said one customer, while another person who deemed it a closet “essential” gushed about how “soft,” “comfortable,” and “adorable” the style is. It’s also shockingly versatile; I often wear it more casually with a pair of sneakers, but it looks just as good dressed up with heels and a sleek jacket (and layered with a turtleneck during the winter). While the style is available in 23 colorways, I’m personally obsessed with the tartan pattern for late fall and winter, with one shopper deeming it “the perfect holiday dress.” 

The Norah Nap Dress 

While the Ellie Nap Dress might be the brand’s most iconic silhouette, if you prefer shorter dresses, the Norah style is a must. This style hits around the mid-thigh and features slightly puffed long sleeves and small ruffles at the scoop neck. One shopper wrote that it has a “super flattering fit” and looks “cute with high boots” in colder months. Another customer who called it “stylish and comfy” agreed, writing that the dress “looks great with tights and boots and will be on constant rotation in [their] wardrobe this winter.”

The Maude Dress

But if you want a true winter dress, consider the full-length Maude style, which is made from a warm wool-blend. This style is fitted at the top before flaring at the hips, which lead one “very busty” shopper with “larger hips” to write, “I don’t want to take it off!” “The fabric has some stretch to it so it easily accommodates my chest and hips, and the way it clings to my waist is super flattering,” they wrote, calling it “a classic piece that you could have in your wardrobe for years and not get tired of.” And per another fan, “If you're on the fence, get it — it's really a beautiful dress and feels so elegant to wear.”

The Party Platform

And though Hill House Home might be famous for its dresses, shoppers can’t stop raving about the brand’s Party Platform heels, which are available in eight different looks. “These are the most comfortable heels I have ever worn in my entire life,” wrote one customer who was amazed that their “feet didn’t hurt after wearing them all night.” Another person who said that they “never” write reviews made an exception for these “perfect” heels, writing that they “are the best special occasion shoes [they]’ve ever encountered. Comfortable, elegant, flattering and a great price point.”
