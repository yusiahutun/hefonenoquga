Jack The Rippers grave REVEALED: Killer who slayed prostitutes in revenge for sex disease

Publish date: 2024-04-28

David Bullock, 41, has studied the most “viable” suspect Thomas Cutbush since he was a teenager.

He gained access to his files from Broadmoor psychiatric hospital and uncovered Cutbush’s family plot in Nunhead Cemetery, south east London.

Bullock sets out his case in new book, The Man Who Would Be Jack.

He said: “People always said he died in Broadmoor, but looking at the files I could see he wasn’t buried there.

“I found his grave by finding out where his other family members were buried, thinking maybe they were buried in the same place.

“He is buried in Nunhead Cemetery – formerly named All Saints – which was known as the Dead Cemetery because it was one of the Magnificent Seven cemeteries created to bury the dead in London and it was left to become overgrown and woodland.”

Bullock said Cutbush’s grave is now covered in ivy and bramble.

Over a hundred suspects have been named but Bullock believes only a few are viable.

He added: “Cutbush worked in Whitechapel at the time and knew the area like the back of his hand; he had a hatred towards prostitutes; and his family and friends believe he was linked to this because he had an obsession with medicine and surgery and anatomy.

“He studied medical books and drew pictures of mutilation and would tell people he was a doctor when he wasn’t.”

Cutbush also used prostitutes and claimed to have caught a disease from one of them.

He then self-medicated and ended up with a disfigurement.

Cutbush was eventually arrested in 1891 for attacking two women and the series of murders stopped.

Jack The Ripper killed five or six prostututes in Whitechapel, east London, in 1888.

Mary Jane Kelly is widely believed to be the final victim of the notorious serial killer.

The 25-year-old was killed by a slash to the throat and left with mutilations “that would have taken two hours to perform”.
