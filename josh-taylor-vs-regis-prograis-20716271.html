Josh Taylor vs Regis Prograis prize money for World Boxing Super Series fight

Publish date: 2024-06-08

Josh Taylor and Regis Prograis will battle it out for a £4m prize fund as well as the Muhammad Ali trophy this weekend.

The lucrative World Boxing Super Series continues to reward fighters with big pay cheques.

Both Taylor and Prograis have already collected over £1m so far in making their way to this season’s light-welterweight decider.

But the extra incentive to lift the trophy as well as becoming a unified champion is the huge cash prize on offer.

WBSS promised big money prizes when it started in 2017 with a prize pot of around £38m.

That figure has been slightly dropped this season while the move to three weights rather than two has meant the money is more spread out.

But it is still huge money for the two fighters to be earning consider their previous pay packets before entering the tournament wouldn’t have come close.

The prize fund on offer and the Ali trophy means that a fourth judge will be ringside for the fight to decide the winner if the main three officials call it as a draw.

This is standard for all WBSS fights as they do not have rematches to ensure the tournament isn’t held up.

The fourth judge will also score the fight and his card will be counted if the other three cards make it a draw.

There is also a count back system in place if the fourth judge does not split the fighters.

Then whoever won the 12th round will be confirmed the winner and if that is level they go back to the 11th and so forth.

But, crucially, while the fourth judge and countback system decides the WBSS winner it does not have an effect on the boxers’ records.

So if it is a draw on the main three cards then both Taylor and Prograis will remain undefeated and will retain their IBF and WBA light-welterweight titles respectively.
