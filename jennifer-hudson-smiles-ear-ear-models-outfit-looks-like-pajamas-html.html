Jennifer Hudson was pictured while arriving at CBS Mornings' studio in New York City on Monday.

The 42-year-old singer-songwriter flashed a wide smile as she exited a car and posed for a few photos as she spent time in Manhattan's Midtown district.

The performer, who recently dazzled the attendees of a charitable event in Las Vegas, wore a striped green wide-legged and long-sleeve jumpsuit that contrasted well with her dark gray trench coat. 

Hudson also wore a pair of dark brown high-heeled shoes and accessorized with a bright white purse.

The actress' gorgeous dark brunette hair fell towards her shoulders and contrasted well with the light tone of her jumpsuit.

Hudson used her time on the program to speak about The Jennifer Hudson Show, and she began by discussing her recent episode featuring Smokey Robinson.

She stated that being able to have the singer as a guest on her show was 'such a dream. I mean, I love being able to sit with legends like that and just pick their brains and learn as much as I can.'

The actress then gushed about having the opportunity to duet with the hitmaker on her program. 

'To be able to sing with him on my show and have that type of moment, oh my god. It's one of my favorite moments this season,' she said.

Hudson went on to state that, although she was not used to serving as the lead of a program, she was grateful for the support of her fans, guests and team. 

'It's something I'm learning as I go...but I think I thrive off of the energy of the people,' she said.

The performer also opened up about her favorite part of producing her talk program.

She stated that she greatly enjoyed 'celebrating people and making beautiful memories and moments.'

The Jennifer Hudson Show made its debut on September 12, 2022, which also happened to be the performer's 41st birthday.

The first season of the program ran for a total of 186 episodes, and the show's second run premiered last month.

The Jennifer Hudson Show has received mixed to positive reviews from critics ever since its debut.

The project has also been nominated for various Daytime Emmy Awards, and it is currently in the running for the Outstanding Daytime Talk Series distinction.
