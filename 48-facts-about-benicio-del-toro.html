Benicio Del Toro made his acting debut in the film “Big Top Pee-wee” (1988).

This charming comedy marked Del Toro’s first foray into the world of acting. Although his role was small, it was a stepping stone to the successful career that awaited him.

He won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in “Traffic” (2000).

Del Toro’s portrayal of Javier Rodriguez, a Mexican police officer caught up in the world of drug trafficking, garnered widespread acclaim and earned him the prestigious Oscar.

Del Toro is fluent in both English and Spanish.

Being of Puerto Rican descent, Del Toro grew up bilingual and has been able to seamlessly transition between English and Spanish-speaking roles throughout his career.

He appeared in the Marvel Cinematic Universe as The Collector.

In the films “Thor: The Dark World” (2013) and “Guardians of the Galaxy” (2014), Del Toro portrayed the eccentric character known as The Collector, a cosmic Marvel entity who collects rare and unique items.

Del Toro won the Best Actor Award at the Cannes Film Festival for his role in “Che” (2008).

His powerful performance as revolutionary leader Ernesto “Che” Guevara in Steven Soderbergh’s epic biographical drama earned him critical acclaim and international recognition.

He starred alongside Johnny Depp in “Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas” (1998).

In this cult classic film based on Hunter S. Thompson’s novel, Del Toro played the role of Dr. Gonzo, a deeply eccentric and drug-addled companion to Johnny Depp’s character, Raoul Duke.

Del Toro provided the voice for the character of Ernesto de la Cruz in Disney-Pixar’s “Coco” (2017).

His resonant voice lent itself perfectly to the animated character, capturing the essence of a charming and charismatic musician.

He was nominated for an Emmy Award for his portrayal of infamous drug lord Pablo Escobar in the TV series “Escobar: Paradise Lost” (2014).

Del Toro’s mesmerizing performance showcased his ability to delve deep into complex characters and bring them to life on the screen.

Del Toro directed the film “El Yuma” (2010).

Not only has he proven his skills as an actor, but also as a director. “El Yuma” tells the story of a tourist’s encounter with the real Cuba, exploring themes of culture and identity.

He is known for his intensive preparation for roles.

Del Toro immerses himself fully in the characters he portrays, often conducting extensive research and adopting physical and emotional transformations to bring them to life.

Del Toro played a memorable role in “Snatch” (2000).

In Guy Ritchie’s fast-paced crime comedy, Del Toro delivered a memorable performance as the character Franky Four Fingers, showcasing his ability to masterfully balance humor and intensity.

He has collaborated with director Alejandro González Iñárritu on multiple projects.

Del Toro and Iñárritu have worked together on films such as “21 Grams” (2003) and “Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)” (2014), forming a successful creative partnership.

Del Toro is passionate about environmental conservation.

He actively supports initiatives that aim to protect the environment and raise awareness about the importance of sustainable practices.

He played the iconic character of The Wolf in “Pulp Fiction” (1994).

Del Toro’s brief but memorable appearance as the mysterious and calm fixer left a lasting impression on audiences.

Del Toro has been involved in humanitarian work.

He has used his celebrity status to advocate for human rights and support various charitable causes, including organizations focused on education and healthcare.

He is known for his intense and captivating on-screen presence.

Del Toro’s ability to command the attention of audiences with his enigmatic performances has earned him immense praise from critics and his peers.

Del Toro starred in the crime thriller “Sicario” (2015).

In this gripping film, he portrayed the character of Alejandro Gillick, a mysterious and morally ambiguous hitman involved in the war against Mexican drug cartels.

He was a member of the jury at the 2010 Cannes Film Festival.

Del Toro had the honor of being part of the jury that selected the winners of the prestigious film festival, showcasing his expertise and passion for cinema.

Del Toro is known for his unconventional and challenging roles.

He is not afraid to take on complex characters that push the boundaries of traditional storytelling, earning him critical acclaim and respect in the industry.

He has won numerous awards throughout his career.

Del Toro has been recognized for his outstanding performances with accolades such as Golden Globe Awards, BAFTA Awards, and Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Del Toro is a versatile actor who effortlessly transitions between genres.

Whether it’s drama, crime, comedy, or action, Del Toro has showcased his range and ability to adapt to different styles of storytelling.

He starred in the film “The Hunted” (2003) alongside Tommy Lee Jones.

In this intense thriller, Del Toro played the role of an expert tracker who becomes the target of a manhunt led by Tommy Lee Jones’ character.

Del Toro played the character of Taneleer Tivan, also known as The Collector, in the Marvel film “Guardians of the Galaxy” (2014).

His eccentric portrayal of this cosmic character added depth and intrigue to the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

He appeared in the film “21 Grams” (2003) alongside Naomi Watts and Sean Penn.

In this emotionally charged drama, Del Toro delivered a powerful performance as a man whose life intersects with two other characters following a tragic accident.

Del Toro has worked with acclaimed director Denis Villeneuve.

He collaborated with Villeneuve on the films “Sicario” (2015) and “The Wolfman” (2010), showcasing their shared commitment to creating impactful and thought-provoking cinema.

He played the character of Lawrence Talbot in “The Wolfman” (2010).

Del Toro tackled the challenging role of the iconic werewolf, delivering a performance that captured both the torment and savagery of the character.

Del Toro starred in the crime drama “Traffic” (2000).

His portrayal of the conflicted Mexican police officer, Javier Rodriguez, earned him critical acclaim and solidified his status as a talented and versatile actor.

He played a pivotal role in the film “Sin City” (2005).

Del Toro embodied the character of Jackie Boy, a corrupt police officer, in this stylized neo-noir crime thriller based on Frank Miller’s graphic novels.

Del Toro collaborated with director Guillermo del Toro on “The Devil’s Backbone” (2001).

Working with the visionary director, Del Toro contributed to the atmospheric and haunting nature of the film, which is regarded as a modern classic.

He made a memorable appearance in “The Avengers: Infinity War” (2018) and “Avengers: Endgame” (2019) as The Collector.

Del Toro reprised his role as The Collector in these epic Marvel blockbusters, adding depth and intrigue to the storyline.

Del Toro starred in the film “Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas” (1998).

In this psychedelic journey, he portrayed the eccentric and unpredictable Dr. Gonzo alongside Johnny Depp’s character, Raoul Duke, based on Hunter S. Thompson’s novel.

Del Toro has been recognized for his dedication to fully immersing himself in each role, often undergoing physical transformations and intense preparation.

Del Toro’s performance in “Che” (2008) earned him critical acclaim and recognition.

His portrayal of the iconic revolutionary leader won him the Best Actor Award at the Cannes Film Festival, solidifying his place among the industry’s most talented actors.

He has lent his voice to animated films.

Del Toro’s deep and emotive voice has been featured in animated movies such as “The Book of Life” (2014) and “The Little Prince” (2015).

Del Toro co-starred with Emily Blunt in “Sicario” (2015).

Their on-screen chemistry and nuanced performances elevated the intensity of the crime-thriller, leaving a lasting impact on audiences.

He played the character of Ernesto de la Cruz in Disney-Pixar’s “Coco” (2017).

His rich and captivating voice brought to life the beloved musician Ernesto de la Cruz, contributing to the film’s heartfelt storytelling.

Gilliam’s distinct visual style paired with Del Toro’s intense and unhinged performance created a memorable cinematic experience.

Del Toro appeared in the film “The Way of the Gun” (2000).

This gritty neo-noir crime thriller showcased Del Toro’s ability to portray morally ambiguous characters, further solidifying his reputation as an actor willing to take risks.

Del Toro has celebrated his Puerto Rican heritage and has embraced roles that explore the complexity and diversity of different cultures.

Del Toro collaborated with director Paul Thomas Anderson on “Inherent Vice” (2014).

His portrayal of the eccentric maritime lawyer Sauncho Smilax contributed to the bizarre and psychedelic atmosphere of the film.

He has worked with renowned director Steven Soderbergh on multiple occasions.

Their collaborations on “Traffic” (2000) and “Che” (2008) showcased the deep trust and understanding between Del Toro and Soderbergh.

Del Toro’s performance in “21 Grams” (2003) earned him critical acclaim.

His portrayal of a man connected to two other characters through a tragic accident showcased his ability to convey complex emotions and internal turmoil.

He played the character of Dr. Alex Sabian in “The Fan” (1996).

In this psychological thriller, Del Toro starred alongside Robert De Niro, delivering a captivating performance as a dedicated and caring psychologist.

Del Toro played a pivotal role in “The Hunted” (2003).

His performance as an expert tracker in this gripping thriller showcased his ability to bring intensity and depth to every character he portrays.

He has created memorable characters in independent films.

Del Toro has taken on unconventional and challenging roles in independent cinema, bringing authenticity and depth to characters that push the boundaries of traditional storytelling.

Del Toro’s role in “Guardians of the Galaxy” (2014) expanded the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

His character, The Collector, introduced audiences to the cosmic side of the Marvel universe, setting the stage for further exploration of its vast mythology.
