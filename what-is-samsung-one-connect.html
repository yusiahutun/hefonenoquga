What Is Samsung One Connect? Can You Watch a Samsung TV Without It?

Publish date: 2024-08-01

Samsung's televisions are hugely popular worldwide, offering high-grade TVs in various sizes and prices, with many models delivering incredibly vivid and detailed pictures. But your Samsung TV may be missing something called a One Connect box. So, what is One Connect, and is it required to use your Samsung TV?

What Is Samsung One Connect?

Nowadays, people often go beyond simply plugging their televisions into a satellite box. People connect laptops, game consoles, and other hardware to their TVs, resulting in tangled cords. This can be incredibly frustrating and presents an electric shock and fire hazard. Such tangles can even damage your cables beyond repair. But how can you keep your cables tidy?

This is where Samsung One Connect comes in. One Connect is a media receiver and TV tuner that can streamline the organization of your cables to minimize tangles and, therefore, hazards or malfunctions. As you can see above, the One Connect comes as a long rectangular box with a range of cable inputs.

Newer models of the One Connect come with various important TV connections, such as HDMI 2.1 ports, an Ethernet port, an optical output port, three USB 2.0 ports, an RF antenna input, and an enhanced audio return channel. This allows you to connect many devices to the One Connect box and, in turn, to your television.

You can place your One Connect box wherever you like—behind your TV, on the wall underneath (if mounted), or elsewhere nearby.

But there's no denying that the typical One Connect box is bulky and hard to store in smaller places. Nowadays, many people appreciate minimalism and don't want their devices to be overly large or aesthetically displeasing.

So, in 2021, Samsung released the Slim Connect Box. As the name suggests, this is a slimmer, sleeker version of the One Connect box that is much easier on the eye. In addition, with its reduced size, the Slim Connect Box is easier to tuck away behind your television if desired.

The Slim Connect box contains numerous connections, including three USB ports, one optical port, one HDMI port, one WLAN port, two satellite ports, and one antenna port.

Samsung also offers a Mini One Connect box. This box has four HDMI input ports and a single One Connect port. This is obviously more limited than the One Connect and Slim Connect but is well suited to more basic needs or tight spaces.

But do you need a One Connect box at all? Is it a requirement for all Samsung TVs (and some monitor models) or simply a convenient add-on?

Do You Need a Samsung One Connect Box?

In short, no. You do not have to have a One Connect box to use your Samsung TV. This device has gained popularity among Samsung TV owners because it is so beneficial in keeping cables tidy. So, worry not. You can still use a Samsung TV without adding a One Connect box.

But a One Connect could be useful, especially if your television is connected to multiple devices simultaneously. As mentioned, a heap of tangled cables can be dangerous and look unsightly. A One Connect box could help you clean up your cables and steer clear of hazards.

If you think that you'd benefit from a One Connect box, you'll likely be able to find one easily online. Prices for the One Connect range between $80 and $300, depending on the version and condition of the device.

You can grab a One Connect box for a lower price if it's used, refurbished, or certified pre-owned. If you're working with a budget, consider opting for a used or refurbished unit to save a decent chunk of cash (so long as you can trust the seller).

Samsung One Connect boxes are available on Amazon, eBay, AliExpress, OnBuy, and various other retailers. So, if you're interested in getting one of these handy devices, check out one of these sites to see what's on offer.

Samsung One Connect Is Useful—But Not Vital

While using a Samsung One Connect box can be a big help, it is by no means essential for using your television. Check out One Connect if you're interested in what it can offer, but remember that if you don't feel that you need it, it will not affect your Samsung TV experience.
