Serena Williams's Hubby Launches Fellowship to Combat Climate Change in Memory of Late Mum

Publish date: 2024-06-22
  • Serena Williams's husband Alexis Ohanian has launched a KSh 2 billion Fellowship to combat climate change, which mostly affects people in marginalised areas
  • According to the founder of Reddit Inc, the Fellowship is in memory of his mother who died of brain cancer 14 years ago
  • The Fellowship is open to young people aged between 18-23 and ready to dedicate the next two years to build on the forefront of climate innovation

Serena Williams's Husband Alexis Ohanian Sr. has unveiled a KSh 2 billion ($20 million) Fellowship to combat climate change in honour of his late mum.

Mum died 14 years ago

Ohanian's mum died 14 years ago of terminal brain cancer just months into creating his successful internet company, Reddit, Inc.

"I'm tired of this day being a sad day for me and our family, so I'm launching the 776 Foundation (mission: fight inequity worldwide) so we can always remember March 15, as the day I began a legacy of good work to honour her," said Ohanian Sr.

In a LinkedIn post, Ohanian says he didn't want to wait until he was old to put his experience, network, and wealth to work.

To address climate change

"I’m committing KSh 2 billion ($20million ) to our first program, the 776 Fellowship to address the greatest threat to our future -climate change," said the innovator.

The two-year 776 Fellowship program will equip 20 dedicated, young leaders in the climate space with a KSh 11 million ($100,000) grant and the full support from the Foundation’s network of founders, investors, and partners.

"This new generation has grown up in the great shadow of climate change and has proven again and again that they are driven to find real solutions to build a better future," said the techpreneur.

Applications are now open

The 776 Foundation Fellowship Program will be helmed by a very familiar face within the Seven Seven Six community: Elisabeth Garvin. The latter is Ohanian's Founding Chief of Staff, and she will take up the role of Foundation & Fellowship Program Director.

Applications for the inaugural class of the 776 Foundation Fellowship are now open.

If you’re between 18-23 and ready to dedicate the next two years to build on the forefront of climate innovation, join us on this critical mission.

Kenya's resilience to climate change

Tuko.co.ke previously reported that The World Bank approved a KSh 16.6 million loan to strengthen Kenya's resilience to climate change.

The World Bank said the money will be used in funding local climate change projects in rural areas under the Financing Locally–Led Climate Action.

The new programme will support community-identified and locally-led climate resilience projects in all rural wards in Kenya.
