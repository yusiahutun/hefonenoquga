From His Collegiate Career To NBA

Publish date: 2024-09-28

Shake Milton, aka Malik Benjamin Milton, is an American professional basketball player who plays as a shooting guard for the Philadelphia 76ers of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

Milton, the Georgia native, played professional basketball at Owasso High School and SMU.

During his high school career, the shooting guard earned numerous honors. The awards committee named him Tulsa World Player of the Year, Oklahoma Super 5 Player of the Year, and Oklahoma Coaches Association ALL-STATE.

In his college career, he was a member of the US Select Team for the Goodwill Tour in Croatia and was named AAC All-Rookie First Team and All-American Athletic Conference Second Team.

Milton started his NBA journey with the Philadelphia 76ers even though the Dallas Mavericks selected him with the 54th overall pick in the 2018 NBA draft.

Early Life, Parents, And Education

Malik Benjamin Milton was born on 26 September 1996 in Savannah, Georgia, to Myron Milton and Lisa Milton. His father, Myron, used to coach basketball at AAU.

Myron always wanted his son to become a basketball player and now Malik has achieved his dream.

But sadly, he was not able to see his son play in the NBA, as he passed away when Malik was about 11 years old.

Myron had an abnormal enlargement of cardiomyopathy, a heart disease that causes heart complications to pump blood efficiently.

Shake’s mother, Lisa Milton, used to work in Savannah to fulfill her children’s needs. Milton has one brother, Myron Milton Jr., and two sisters, Terryn Milton and Valanette Amie.

In an interview with NBC Sports, she said, “When he (Malik) was inside my belly, the doctor asked about him, and I said the child shakes a little. So after that, we decided to put his nickname Shake.”

Education

Milton completed his primary level education from his hometown’s school. He started playing basketball for his school team as a small child.

The shooting guard moved to Owasso High School for further studies. After finishing high school in 2015, he committed to Southern Methodist University despite getting an invitation from the University of Oklahoma.

However, there is not much information available about his field of study.

Career

Milton played basketball for the school’s team during his childhood. After seeing his potential, his father decided to join him at Owasso High School, where he could improve his skills.

At Owasso, Shake played for three seasons, and in his senior year, he logged an average of 29.7 points and 4.4 assists per game. This record was quite an impressive one for a high school player.

During his time with his high school team, he received the Gatorade Player of the Year awards and was named Tulsa World Player of the Year, Oklahoma Super 5 Player of the Year, and Oklahoma Coaches Association ALL-STATE.

College Career

In 2016, Milton moved to Southern Methodist University, Dallas, Texas, and played for the Mustangs. He played his debut for the team in a match against Oklahoma University’s team.

Shake earned AAC All-Rookie First Team in his first season after logging an average of 10.5 points, 3.0 rebounds, and 2.7 assists in 30 games.

Furthermore, Milton traveled to Croatia with the US Select Team in a Goodwill Tour to play friendly basketball in 2016.

In his second season, Milton played 35 games and recorded an average of 13.0 points, 4.5 assists, and 1.3 steals per game. He also made the All-American Athletic Conference Second Team as a sophomore.

The following season, he averaged 18.0 points, 4.7 rebounds, 4.4 assists, and 1.4 steals per game and made it to the All-AAC Second Team.

NBA Career

After performing impressively in college basketball, the Dallas Mavericks drafted Milton as the 54th overall pick in the 2018 NBA draft.

However, the Mavericks traded him with the Philadelphia 76ers for the rights to the 56th pick, Ray Spalding, and the 60th pick, Kostas Antetokounmpo.

After that, Milton signed a two-way contract with the 76ers and started playing for their G League affiliate, the Delaware Blue Coats.

Milton debuted in the NBA on 30 November 2018, in a game against the Washington Wizards, and scored five points and provided two assists.

Milton appeared in 20 games that season and had an average of 4.4 points per game, 0.9 assists, and 1.8 rebounds.

The 2019-20 season started on a good note as he signed a four-year deal with the 76ers. However, he missed the early game due to his injury.

He made his first career start on January 25, 2020, against the Los Angeles Lakers. In March 2020, he scored a career-high 39 points in a 136-130 loss to the Los Angeles Clippers.

Milton is not performing well since the 2021-22 season, as he has just managed to maintain an average of 8.2 points that season and 8.5 points per game in the following season.
