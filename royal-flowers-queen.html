13 best flowers named after the royal family

Publish date: 2024-08-05

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Clematis 'Prince Charles'

Clematis 'Prince Charles'

These striking pale purple/blue flowers are named in honour of King Charles, who is known to enjoy gardening as a pastime. Native to New Zealand, this climber plant typically blooms between July and September.

Rosa 'The Queen Elizabeth'

Rosa 'The Queen Elizabeth'

Now 35% Off

Named in honour of Queen Elizabeth II, this beautiful blush pink rose dates back to the early 1950s when the royal ascended the British throne. A true classic, these lovely blooms will inject some warmth and feminine charm wherever they’re planted.

'Elizabeth' Clematis

'Elizabeth' Clematis

Another flower for the late Queen, with large pink petals and a soft yellow centre. Regarded as the 'Queen of Climbers', clematis is suited to growing up walls, fences and trees – the ideal option if you’re looking to create a striking border in your garden.

'Queen Victoria' Lobelia Cardinalis Seeds

'Queen Victoria' Lobelia Cardinalis Seeds

Named after the 19th-century monarch, Queen Victoria, these fiery red cardinal flowers are simply stunning. Striking and bold, they will add an instant wow-factor to any outdoor space.

'Princess Diana' Clematis

'Princess Diana' Clematis

The late Princess Diana has numerous flowers dedicated to her, including this stunning clematis plant. It blooms from early summer to autumn, so you can still enjoy its magenta, tulip-shaped flowers as the seasons change.

'Prince William' Clematis

'Prince William' Clematis

Like his father, mother and grandmother, the Prince of Wales has also been honoured with a clematis plant. This time, it bears distinct violet and burgundy petals and flowers between July and September.

'Platinum Jubilee' Rose

'Platinum Jubilee' Rose

Now 17% Off

The Yorkshire-based nursery Harkness Roses created this vibrant rose to celebrate Queen Elizabeth II’s Platinum Jubilee last year. It's layered with magenta and blush pink petals and has a lingering fragrance that's powerful yet sweet.

'Duke of Edinburgh' Rose

'Duke of Edinburgh' Rose

To mark what would have been the Duke’s 100th birthday, this double-flowered rose was unveiled by the Royal Horticultural Society (RHS) in June 2021. Dark pink in colour, it’ll look a treat in any rose garden or rose bed.

'Queen Mum' Agapanthus

'Queen Mum' Agapanthus

This delicate white-flowered plant is dedicated to the late Queen Mother, who passed away in 2002. Blooming between July and August, the African lily has dainty trumpet-shaped flowers that boast a hint of violet at their centre.

We think these vibrant flowers perfectly encapsulate Queen Elizabeth’s sister Princess Margaret, who was known for being sociable and vivacious. A vivid mix of rich red, orange and yellow tones makes for a real show-stopping display that’s sure to inject some colour into your space.

'Diana's Memory' Dahlia

'Princess Anne' Shrub Rose
