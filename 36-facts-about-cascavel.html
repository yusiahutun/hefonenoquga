Cascavel is the third-largest city in the state of Paraná.

FAQs

Q: What is the population of Cascavel?
A: The population of Cascavel is approximately 320,000 people.

Q: What is the main economic activity in Cascavel?
A: Agriculture is the main economic activity in Cascavel, with a focus on soybean and poultry production.

Q: What is the climate like in Cascavel?
A: Cascavel has a subtropical climate, with hot summers and mild winters. Average temperatures range from 20°C (68°F) in summer to 10°C (50°F) in winter.

Q: Are there any tourist attractions in Cascavel?
A: Yes, Cascavel boasts several tourist attractions, including the Municipal Theater, the Municipal Museum, and the Itaipu Hydroelectric Power Plant.

Q: Is Cascavel a safe city?
A: Cascavel has a relatively low crime rate compared to other cities in Brazil. However, it is always advisable to take basic precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Q: What is the local cuisine like in Cascavel?
A: The local cuisine in Cascavel is influenced by traditional Brazilian dishes, with an emphasis on barbecue, feijoada (a black bean stew), and various meat and fish dishes.
