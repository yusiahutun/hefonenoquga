Lie detector tests (also known as “Polygraph tests”) are one of the cutting-edge forensic scientific technologies that have proven to be extremely useful during police investigations. On one hand, some view this approach as a harsh and demeaning investigation technique, while on the other hand, others consider it useful for investigating serious or vicious crimes. Believe it or not, when applying for certain jobs, individuals may undergo lie detector or polygraph tests, such as those required for government positions with the FBI or CIA.

The goal of a lie detector test, as the name implies, is to determine whether a person is telling the truth or lying when answering questions. However, the administration of this test differs from narcoanalysis and brain mapping. But, in India, are they legal? This is the main topic of conversation. The following sensors are recorded in the Polygraph test:
  • Person’s breathing rate
  • Person’s pulse
  • Person’s blood pressure
  • Person’s perspiration (sweat)
  • Sometimes, we also record arm or leg movements.

When the polygraph test begins, the questioner asks a few questions to establish the norms for the person’s signals before moving on to genuine questions. The interrogation records each of the person’s signals on the moving paper.

So, during or after the polygraph test, a polygraph examiner looks at the graph to see if the vital signs changed considerably on any question. A faster heart rate, greater blood pressure, and increased sweating or perspiration, for example, all suggest that the person is lying.
