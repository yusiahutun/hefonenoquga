'Amsterdam dad glory hole blunder' is a fake as THOSE viral voicenotes debunked

Publish date: 2024-09-11

A viral voicenote about an 'incestuous glory hole blunder' in Amsterdam's famous Red Light District has been debunked as fake after the story left Brits "rattled" this week.

Anyone unlucky enough to hear the message — regardless of whether it came up on their social media feeds or if they searched for it out of curiousity — will have been wishing they could unhear it over the alleged sickening encounter between a father and daughter. In the viral screen recording, a woman claimed a joint stag and hen do apparently had to be called off early after a mystery sex act went badly wrong.

According to voice messages shared on social media, the the stag and hen do from same wedding were apparently in Amsterdam for a few nights of frivolity and pre-nuptial partying . But things went wrong after one of the revellers performed oral sex and got the biggest shock of all time.

"One of the girls was super drunk and went into one of these booths with like a glory hole," the recording says after acknowledging the person speaking heard it through the grapevine. At this point, a bloke on the other side stuck his todger through.

The girl then supposedly performed a sex act on the bloke. They were allegedly designed in such a way that if a button was pressed by the people on each side then the window between them would turn translucent and each of them would be able to see who was on the other side. She did it at the end... and the the bloke on the side was said to have been her dad!

Anyone in their right mind felt ill after hearing the mortifying story which was "ripping the family apart" - but thankfully, none of it was real. According to an obviously-vital investigation by Vice, a reporter went to Amsterdam to conduct an investigation into the city's glory holes and found no evidence of any such place where people could press a button to reveal who was on the other side.

The publication also claims to called 'every glory hole operator in Amsterdam' and found that most of the venues would turn women away. But those that did let women in said they didn't have a screen with a button.
