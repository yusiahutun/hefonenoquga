Kendall Jenner's Red Knit Bikini Is Making Us Long for Summer

Publish date: 2024-07-05

Props to Kendall Jenner for escaping the bitter winter and traveling to the tropics to kick off the new year.

The 28-year-old model shared a new slideshow to Instagram, where she showed off a teeny knit bikini courtesy of Deparel. The reversible, two-toned set included thin vermilion straps, with the halter-style bikini top and matching thong bottoms featuring contrasting wavy white stripes. According to the brand, this micro knitkini is from the Swim 2024 collection and is made from a water-resistant knit elastane textile—which means Jenner needn’t worry if she wants to take a dip in the sea. On the Deparel site, the set is available in coral, kelp, and sea foam color options.

After she shared the stunning swimsuit photos, the comments were flooded with glowing remarks from family members, like her younger sister Kylie, who wrote, “HOT,” and older sister Khloé Kardashian, who commented, “Oh my lord! Happy new year to all of us.”

This wasn’t the only Deparel swimsuit Jenner wore on her holiday. In a series of snaps shared to the design studio’s official Instagram, she is seen sporting another similar knitkini set, this time in tones of yellow and green, with large beaded accessories on the straps. This swimwear was from Deparel’s 2021 archival silhouette release, and it is now available to shop on the company’s website as well.

It has been quite a stylish few days for Jenner, who shared another beachside photo shoot earlier this week, in which she wears a flowy cream gown from Helsa, featuring ruffled hems and a diamond cutout on the waist, which she paired with glass teardrop earrings from Bottega Veneta. In the caption, the 818 Tequila founder wrote, “Years pass, and they seem faster and faster. presence and time is all i can ask for. so grateful for what has past, so optimistic for what’s to come. go love on the people you hold close to your heart, and don’t waste a second ❤️ 2024, shower me with love and laughter, gratitude and growth, patience and presence.”
