How to make coloured eyeliner work for you

Publish date: 2024-03-07

Although coloured eyeliner might not feature on many desert island packing lists, for those in the know it’s a make-up bag staple for myriad reasons.

Should you want to experiment with Euphoria-esque trends, pops of rainbow bright liner are the easiest way in. Perhaps you need an easy, chic evening look? A smudge of conker brown or glimmery grey will enhance smoky eyes in a flash. Or, if you’re looking to shake up your everyday make-up without much effort, a wash of indigo, olive or maroon tracing the top or lower lash lines will promptly do so.

Indeed, coloured eyeliner is quite the make-up stalwart, whether your vibe is minimalist or maximalist, playful or refined. Take it from Victoria Beckham, who can often be seen sporting navy, silver or gold. “Just as in fashion, I like to play with colour and finish to update my beauty wardrobe,” she tells Bazaar. It’s why she launched her highly pigmented, smooth-as-suede Satin Kajal Liners into Victoria Beckham Beauty back when the brand was born in 2019.

The transformation you can achieve from coloured eyeliner, she says, aligns with “colour theory, essentially,” also noting that they are the most versatile product in her own make-up bag, enabling her to achieve looks to amplify her colourings as well as match her mood. Beckham and her team designed the liners with this perspective in play, she adds. “Sure, there is a wide range of colours beyond black, but they’re all modern neutrals, and are meant to contrast and enhance your own eye colour.”

Fast forward to autumn 2022 and Beckham has curated glittery versions. “Our Jewel collection is my take on grown-up glimmer,” she tells us. “Inspired by the intersection of glamour and grunge, each shade projects show-stopping shine for eyes that you can spot at a party, or across the room.” They impart micro-fine – yet unmistakable – sparkle derived from mineral-based silica, dispensed within the brand’s signature creamy texture. “I like to say these are statement glimmer with VB glamour.”

So, how does she wear them? “During the day, I’ll typically use them solo, lining the top of my lids for that glamourous-yet-understated look,” she explains. However, if she’s going for a smoky eye or an all-out statement, Beckham layers them over eye shadow for added dimension.

Her favourite Jewel liner is Gold Lamé, “a pure, striking gold shade” that she says makes a sophisticated accessory to any make-up look. Gold is one of those colours that goes with everything, she points out, “while simultaneously, it’s the star of the show making everything else pop”. To complete the look, “I’ll always balance it with a nude lip,” she adds.

Victor VIRGILE//Getty Images

If glittery gold feels too bold for you, the shimmery Night Flash is what Beckham recommends for a safe eyeliner update this approaching party season. “It’s a slate black colour contrasted with silver glimmer – an easy switch if you don’t want to stray too far from black.”

Perhaps, though, the most wearable – and universally flattering – eyeliner shade is brown. It's ideal if you want to dip your toe in the coloured liner look. “A rich brown is such an underrated neutral and is perfect for that less-dramatic-but-still-impactful eye look,” feels Beckham. “Cocoa is truly a shade that complements not only all skin tones but all eye colours too.”
