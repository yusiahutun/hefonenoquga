Mammito Urges Mike Sonko Not to Be Angry with Conjestina Achieng: &quot;You Can't Put Limits&quot;

Publish date: 2024-07-02
  • Comedian Mammito defended retired boxer Conjestina Achieng from Mike Sonko’s anger
  • Sonko has been trying to help Conjestina with her mental health issues but warned that this is her last chance
  • Mammito educated Sonko on the complexity and sensitivity of mental health issues and urged him to show kindness

Comedian Eunice Mammito has come to the defence of retired boxer Conjestina Achieng, who has been struggling with mental health issues for years.

Mammito asked former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko to be patient and compassionate as he supports Conjestina’s recovery.

Sonko has been one of the prominent figures who have tried to help Conjestina, who was once a celebrated champion in the ring.

Why did Sonko give Conjestina last chance?

He has admitted her to Mombasa Women Rehabilitation Centre, where she is expected to receive professional care from a team of experts.

However, Sonko also expressed his frustration and disappointment with Conjestina’s condition, saying that this was the third and final time he was giving her a chance to get better.

He warned that if she left the rehab centre before completing her treatment and returned to her village, he would not help her again.

“Even though our chances in Conjestina’s healing journey were watered down, I’m a person who doesn’t give up in life. It is for this reason that we have given Conjestina the third and last chance again on her case. I just want to let the public know that she was readmitted to the Mombasa Women Rehabilitation Centre where she will start her treatment afresh under the care of a group of specialists, including councillors, consultants and physiatrists,” Sonko tweeted.

Mammito's message to Sonko

Mammito took a serious tone and responded to Sonko’s tweet, urging him not to be angry with Conjestina.

She explained that mental illness is a complex and challenging problem that requires time and understanding to overcome.

She said that Sonko should not impose limits or deadlines on Conjestina’s healing process, but rather show kindness and love.

“Mental illness is intense and takes time for someone to fully recover… it’s a process and you can’t put limits on when someone will fully recover. Her willingness to go to rehab is a step, so be kind. Let her heal at her own pace! Don’t be harsh where kindness and love are needed,” Mammito noted.
