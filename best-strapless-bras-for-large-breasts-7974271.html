The 11 Best Strapless Bras for Large Breasts of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Publish date: 2024-08-14

It’s no secret that behind every remarkable outfit is a well-rounded selection of undergarments. And on the short list of styles that are an absolute must, a strapless bra is somewhat close to the top. From plunging jumpsuits to backless gowns, the outfits that we wear for special occasions and formal events nearly always call for a strapless bra, making the stakes even higher. No one wants a shoulder strap or band popping out in photos. Yet those with larger breasts can often feel like they have to compromise on style, as strapless bras are notorious for falling down and being all around uncomfortable.

After speaking to bra fit experts, we realized strapless bras don't have to let larger chests down. With the right fit and features, a strapless bra can support even the largest cup sizes. To help you in your search, we tested 15 different strapless bras over the course of two weeks. Each bra was worn by two testers, including at least one with larger breasts (DD+). After evaluating the feedback on the comfort, fit, support, and durability of each bra, we narrowed down our list to the strapless bras that provided the best support for those with larger breasts.

With detachable straps and an undetectable look, this might be the perfect strapless bras for larger chests, except we wish it came in more than four shades to suit a wider range of skin tones.

What to Keep in Mind

Sizing

From the width of your back – aka the band size – to the cups, finding your correct size is key.

“A strapless bra will feel a little different since it doesn’t have the straps, so you need to ensure that it isn’t slipping down or moving around and isn’t creating a bulge over the top or sides,” says fit expert Caroline Peaslee. “First, get yourself measured or measure yourself, and then try on a variety of styles. Choose the one that gives you the look you want, fits well, and feels most comfortable. If after trying on your true size, you find that it doesn’t fit, work with a fit specialist to help you with a different size.”

Trying it on IRL, when possible, is ideal, but those shopping online should make note of the brand’s size guide – that way you can determine whether sizing up or down is required. 

Cup Shape

Before fully committing to a style, one must first consider the shape that they’re going for; which is often determined by the type of clothing you’ll be wearing it with. For those that want the perfect underpinnings for a low-cut gown or top, a style with a plunging neckline, like Cosabella Never Say Never Plungie Strapless Bra, will be better than one that offers full coverage, like ThirdLove 24/7 Classic Strapless Bra. Alternatively, if your outfit is low-cut in the back, go with a backless bra like Felina Longline Backless Bra.

Comfort

While function and fit are of top priority, the way the strapless bra feels is just as important; and in a lot of cases, they go hand in hand. In order to find a style that’s as comfortable as it is supportive, you’ll have to pay close attention to not only the sizing but what the bra is made from. Look for styles that are made with memory foam cups, padded sides, and mesh panels to keep you cool. For instance, we loved how the material in the Aerie Sunnie Strapless Lightly Lined Bra was so breathable, it kept us sweat-free while we danced the night away.

Your Questions, Answered

Is it better to size down in a strapless bra?

When it comes to finding your perfect fit in a strapless bra, there’s no one size fits all solution. But contrary to popular belief, sizing down is not the answer. “Always go with your true size,” says Peaslee. “Never try to adjust the work and function of a bra by sizing up or sizing down. The bra should be working for you, not the other way around.”

How do you prevent a strapless bra from falling down?

According to Peaslee, preventing your strapless bra from sliding down requires you to do your due diligence and find your perfect fit. “Start with the right size,” she advises. “Look for styles that have side boning and/or a silicone grip strip at the top to help keep it in place.” A wider band, like in Wacoal Red Carpet Convertible Strapless Bra, also helps a strapless bra stay up, especially on those with larger chests.
