Naked man filmed being rescued from 'sensual massage parlour' window ledge

Publish date: 2024-09-05

Police were called to “Simply Delicious” kebab shop in Northenden, Manchester, on Monday after receiving calls from neighbours reporting a naked man “holding on to a shop sign”.

Neighbours made the 999 calls to police after the unnamed, naked man was photographed holding his manhood.

The footage shows one firefighter helping the hapless bloke down from the precipice.

The window ledge sits outside Ladybirds massage parlour – which is above the kebab shop.

It is not clear why he was found on the ledge.

One eyewitness said: “I couldn't believe it when I saw him on the ledge.

"He seemed to be holding onto the kebab shop sign but was stuck.

"Police turned up with a battering ram but it wasn't needed.

"The firefighters were trying to get him to come down the ladder but he seemed a bit reluctant.

"He was shouting: 'No, no' and seemed really out of it.

"But he came down in the end."

Another added: "It was crazy - the guy seemed to have climbed out."

A worker from the massage parlour denied knowing anything about the incident, after being confronted by The Sun.

She said: "We weren't on that day - we work different shifts."

Greater Manchester Police detained the man and took him to hospital after firefighters handed him in.

A spokeswoman for the force said the man is now being assessed.

A Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue Service spokesman said: “We were called to this incident at 3.09pm.

"A fire engine from Withington attended the incident and helped one male down from on top of shop signage by using a 9m ladder.

According to the message house’s website, Ladybirds is a “bijoux parlour” and “retains the welcoming and discrete atmosphere that ensures clients return time and time again.”

The self-proclaimed “height of class and sophistication” service, charges clients £50 for 30 minutes, or £90 for two girls during a half an hour slot of a "seductive and sensual massage”.
