Lance Stroll Biography, Age, Height, Girlfriend, Net Worth

Publish date: 2024-08-26

Lance Stroll is a speed racing driver. He competes for Formula One (F1), under the Canadian flag, for Racing Point. Earlier in 2017 and 2018, he has driven for the Williams Racing team as a development driver. He is a resident of Canada belonging to Belgian ancestry. In 2014, he was the Italian Formula 4 Champion. Later in 2015, he became Toyota Racing Series Champion. Subsequently, he became the 2016 FIA Formula 3 European champion.

Lance was born in Montreal, Quebec, Canada on 29th October 1998. His father is Lawrence Stroll, a billionaire Canadian businessman. His mother is Claire-Anne Stroll, a Belgian fashion designer who runs a fashion business named Callens. Stroll has one elder sister, Chloe Stroll. Education details of Lance are not yet revealed by him. In 2010, when he was merely 11 years old, he became a member of the Ferrari Driver Academy for five years.

Lance Stroll Career

At the age of 10, the Belgian-Canadian began his motorsport career in karting. Later in 2009, he became the driver of the year. In 2010, the Ferrari Driver Academy signed him. Stroll made his car racing debut against future Formula One competitors Max Verstappen and Nicholas Latifi in the 2014 Florida Winter Series, a non-championship series organized by the Ferrari Driver Academy. In the 2014 Italian Formula 4 Championship, he made his competitive car racing debut, driving for Prema Powerteam. He became the series champion, recording seven race wins, thirteen podium finishes, and five pole positions, despite he missed the final round due to injury.

In 2015, he was a contestant in the FIA Formula 3 European Championship with Prema Powerteam. His father, Lawrence had taken a stake in that event. In the 33-race series, he won one race at the Hockenheimring and achieved six total podium finishes by competing against future Formula One competitors George Russell, Antonio Giovinazzi, Alexander Albon, and Charles Leclerc. It was declared that Stroll would leave the Ferrari Driver Academy on November 11, 2015, to serve as a test driver for the Williams team.

Lance became the first Canadian Formula One driver since the 1997 World Driver’s Champion Jacques Villeneuve, after driving for the Williams Racing team for the 2017 Formula One season. 2018 was a rough start for Stroll, with numerous mistakes and incidents within the first few races. He failed to score in Canada, his home race where he crashed with Brendon Hartley; scored his next point at Monza despite the 8th place in Azerbaijan. In 2020, Stroll tested positive for COVID-19 and was quarantined for 10 days. After completing the mandated 10 day quarantine period, he was able to take part in the next round, the Portuguese Grand Prix.
