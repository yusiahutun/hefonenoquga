Maine brother of teen who killed their parents speaks out

Publish date: 2024-08-30

The brother of a 17-year-old charged with butchering their parents in a Halloween massacre at their home has spoken of the slayings for the first time.

Christopher Balcer was spared by his younger brother Andrew who let him run out of their house in Winthrop, Maine, after stabbing their parents Ali and Antonio to death on October 31 last year.

The 25-year-old ran to a neighbor's house to call 911 and officers soon arrested Andrew at the family's house.

Now 18, he is awaiting trial for double homicide. 

Christopher, who has been living with his grandparents since the atrocity, broke his silence on Sunday to describe the hatred he now feels for his younger sibling.

Christopher Balcer (right) said he feels nothing but hatred for his younger brother Andrew (left with their mother Ali) who butchered their parents but spared him on Halloween 

'As far as I'm concerned, he died too that night. Hatred is a strong word but it's an accurate one. That's all that's left,' he told The Kennebec Journal.

Christopher said he had no idea what prompted his brother's killing spree. He said he awoke in the middle of the night to a 'commotion' upstairs.

'I went to check and I saw my father lying on his back in a pool of his own blood breathing very slowly and my brother standing over him with a knife.' 

Antonio and Ali Balcer were found dead in pools of their own blood 

Christopher said he then 'pleaded for his life', begging his younger brother to let him go. 

Andrew, who was also armed with their father's gun, allowed it, telling him: 'It's not your day,' he said. 

'I figured it'd be best if I asked him before I took off. When prompted I can run fast but I can't run whatever bullet velocity is and he said "okay, it's not your day. I'm going to call the police." 

'At that point all I can remember thinking is "get out. You have been given a free pass. Get out of there as fast as you can."' 

Christopher ran out through the garage door and fled to a neighbor's house, screaming as he ran down the road in his robe. 

Witnesses mistook his cries for those of a terrorized woman at the time.

No motive for the murders has yet been given. Christopher admitted he didn't know what prompted them but said his brother had been stressed with homework in the weeks beforehand. 

'When he got home, he was doing homework until dinner, then homework until he showered, then he went to bed. He was doing a lot of homework. That was stressing him out.'

Christopher said he has no idea why his younger brother (above with their mother) may have killed them but that Andrew had been stressed about homework before the attacks 

Antonio, 47, suffered 13 stab wounds to the chest. His wife, also 47, was found face down with a stab wound to the back 

Despite considering his brother his 'best friend' until the night of the killings, Christopher said he has no interest in a relationship with him now. 

He said his brother had tried to send him a letter from prison but that he refused to receive it. 

'I wasn't very diplomatic. Basically my response was "choke on it."'

Ali Balcer was found face down in her bedroom with a knife wound to the back. Her husband Antonio had 13 stab wounds to the chest.

The pair, who were both 47 when they died, were popular in the area where they lived. Mrs Balcer was a well-known employee of a local veterinary hospital and Antonio was revered among motorcycle groups. 

He was nicknamed 'The Rev' after officiating weddings in the area. 

Andrew Balcer was taken into custody immediately after the killings. He has since turned 18 and the state is seeking to have him tried as an adult.

He underwent a mental health evaluation in December, the results of which have not been released to the public.

His attorney this week claimed the evaluation was ongoing and would not likely be completed until April.
